What is Semantic Search ?

Semantic search is a search technique that aims to understand the meaning and context of a query and the content being searched, using natural language processing, machine learning, and other artificial intelligence techniques.

Differences between semantic search and traditional search methods :

Semantic search understands the meaning and context of a query and the content being searched, whereas traditional search methods rely on keyword-based matching to find relevant content.

Importance of Semantic Search

Improved search accuracy: Semantic search understands the intent and context of a search query, and can provide more accurate and relevant results.

Better user experience: Semantic search can provide answers to questions, rather than just links to web pages, making the search experience more efficient and enjoyable.

Personalization: Semantic search can provide personalized results based on a user's search history and preferences, improving the overall search experience.

Increased efficiency: Semantic search can save time by providing direct answers to queries, rather than requiring users to sift through search results.

Applications of Semantic Search:

E-commerce: Semantic search can improve product search and recommendation engines, leading to increased sales and revenue

Healthcare: Semantic search can help medical professionals find relevant research papers, clinical trials, and patient information more efficiently.

Customer service: Semantic search can be used to provide more accurate and personalized responses to customer inquiries and support tickets.

Knowledge management: Semantic search can be used to help employees find relevant information and resources more efficiently within a company's knowledge management system.
